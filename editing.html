Module 01 · Video Editing Basics
Interface Overview
Tap each panel to learn what it does.
▶️
Preview Window
Watch your edit update in real-time
+

The Preview Window shows your video exactly as it will look when exported. It updates instantly as you make changes on the timeline. Use Spacebar to play and pause at any time.

Full-screen preview available · Spacebar to play/pause · scrub with the playhead
📂
Media Panel
Import & browse clips, photos, and music
+

This is where all your imported files live. Click Import to bring files from your computer into the project. You can browse, preview, and organize everything here before adding to the timeline.

Supported formats: MP4 · MOV · MKV (video) · PNG · JPG (images) · MP3 · WAV (audio)
🎞️
Timeline
Arrange, trim, and layer all your clips
+

The Timeline is the heart of every edit. Drag clips from the Media Panel here and arrange them in the order you want. You can stack video, audio, and text tracks on separate layers.

Zoom in: Ctrl + =  ·  Zoom out: Ctrl + –  ·  Use this before making precise cuts
🎛️
Properties Panel
Adjust clip settings, effects, speed & volume
+

Select any clip on the timeline and the Properties Panel shows every adjustable setting for that clip: speed, volume, color, effects, position, and more.

All changes apply only to the selected clip. Click a different clip to edit that one instead.
Module 01 · Video Editing Basics
Importing & Timeline
Four steps to get your first edit started.
1
Import Your Media
Click Import in the Media Panel and select files from your computer. Supports MP4, MOV, MKV, PNG, MP3, WAV.
2
Drag to Timeline
Drag clips from the Media Panel down to the Timeline. Arrange them in the order you want them to play.
3
Trim a Clip
Hover over the left or right edge of a clip → drag to shorten or extend it. The Preview Window updates live as you trim.
4
Split a Clip
Move the playhead to the exact cut point. Press Ctrl+B (PC) or Cmd+B (Mac) to split. Delete the unwanted piece.
🚨 Always do this first: Record a 30-second test clip and play it back before every recording session. Catches disasters before they happen.

Adding Transitions
Smooth cuts between clips
+
1
Click the icon between two clips
A small icon appears between adjacent clips on the timeline when you hover.
2
Choose a transition style
Options include Dissolve, Slide, Zoom, Wipe, and many more. Browse the panel that opens.
3
Set the duration
Drag the Duration slider. 0.3–1.5s works well for most ministry content.
4
Apply to All (optional)
Click "Apply to All" to use the same transition between every clip in the timeline.
Ministry rule: 1–2 transition styles per episode maximum. Consistency feels professional; mixing many styles feels chaotic.

Scripture reference, guest name, episode title, or lower-third.

Adjust font, size, color, animation, and position in the right panel.

Set timing on Timeline

💬
Auto Captions
AI generates subtitles from spoken audio automatically
+

CapCut listens to your audio and generates editable captions automatically. This is one of the most powerful tools for ministry content — it makes your videos accessible and effective on social media.

85% of social video is watched on mute. Auto Captions turns your message into readable text for everyone scrolling past. This is not optional for ministry reach.
Module 02 · Audio Editing & Mixing
Music & Volume Levels
Click each topic to expand.
🎵
Adding Background Music
Royalty-free library or upload your own
+
1
Click Audio → Music
Found in the top toolbar of CapCut.
2
Browse or upload
CapCut's royalty-free library or upload your own file from your computer.
3
Drag to Audio Track
Position the music track below your video clips on the timeline.
4
Add Fade In / Fade Out
Right-click the music track → Add Fade. Use 0.5–1s fades to avoid jarring audio cuts at the start and end.
Always check the music license before using in church broadcasts or YouTube uploads.
🔊
Adjusting Volume Levels
Every spoken word must be clearly audible
+
Track TypeRecommended Volume
Speech / Dialogue100%
Background music (with speech)15–30%
Sound effects60–80%
Intro / Outro music (no speech)80–100%
Click a clip → Properties Panel → Volume slider to adjust. If viewers can't hear the speaker, they leave.
🎛️
Keyframe Volume
Raise or lower volume at specific moments
+

Keyframes let you automate volume changes at precise points in the timeline. Use them to duck music under speech, lift it during pauses, or fade it out exactly when needed.

1
Select your audio clip
Click the music or audio track on the timeline.
2
Click the Keyframe button
Found in the Properties Panel. Adds a volume anchor at the playhead position.
3
Move playhead, adjust volume
Drag the playhead to a new position, set a different volume — CapCut creates a smooth curve between them.

Noise Reduction
AI removes background hum and room noise
+

Select your audio clip → Properties Panel → Audio → Noise Reduction. CapCut's AI analyzes and removes consistent background noise — AC hum, fan noise, room echo.

Best for: consistent noise (AC hum, fan). Less effective on: variable noise (traffic, crowd). Always record in the quietest space first.
Noise Reduction is a polish tool — not a rescue tool. Good recording conditions cannot be replaced.
🎵
Beat Sync
Auto-marks music beats as cut points on the timeline
+

Audio → Beat Sync. CapCut analyzes your music and places markers at each beat on the timeline. Great for worship highlight reels and Sunday recap shorts.

Try it with an energetic worship track — the result looks professional with very little effort.
📋 True or False: Noise Reduction can fully fix a recording made in a loud market.
✅ FALSE. Always record in the quietest space available. Noise Reduction helps clean up — it cannot replace good conditions.

📤
How to Export
Step-by-step process
+
1
Click Export (top right)
The Export button is always in the top-right corner of CapCut.
2
Choose Resolution & Quality
Match the settings in the table above for your target platform.
3
Click Export and wait
Do not close CapCut or cancel mid-export. Cancelling creates corrupt files.
4
Save a master copy
Always keep one high-quality export before resizing or reformatting for different platforms.
💡 Always export 1080p minimum for YouTube. Low-res video looks unprofessional on large screens and TVs.
Module 03 · Exporting & Delivery
Sharing to Platforms
Platform-specific best practices for DWC content.
▶️
YouTube
  • Export 1080p MP4 minimum
  • Add title, description & chapter timestamps
  • Schedule for Sunday morning release
  • Custom thumbnail: 1280 × 720
📱
Instagram / Reels
  • Export vertical 9:16 for Reels & Stories
  • Direct Share to Instagram from CapCut
  • Keep Reels under 90 seconds for reach
  • Add captions — 85% watch on mute
📘
Facebook
  • Upload native video, not a YouTube link
  • 1080p MP4 performs best
  • Square or landscape both work well
  • Schedule via Facebook Creator Studio

Practice
Podcast Editing Challenges
Choose your level. Complete it. Show the facilitator when done.
Beginner ✂️
Clean the Conversation
Import a raw podcast recording. Remove at least 5 filler words ("um", "uh", "like") by splitting and deleting. Keep the audio feeling natural — no jarring jumps.
💡 Zoom into the timeline waveform to find the exact start and end of each filler word.
Intermediate ✏️
Build the Episode Open
Create a 15–30 second branded intro: (1) title card with episode name, (2) background music fade-in, (3) lower-third with the host's name.
💡 Use Auto Captions on the host introduction for accessibility.
Advanced 🚀
Full Episode Edit
Edit a 3–5 minute podcast from raw footage: remove dead air, add B-roll over two jump cuts, balance audio levels, add episode title text, and export 1080p for YouTube.
💡 Export 1080p MP4. Add YouTube chapter markers in the description for easy navigation.
